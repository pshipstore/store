Rakhi/Rakshabandhan

The celebration of the eternal bond between a sister and her brother is best exemplified by Rakshabandhan, the unique festival of India that is celebrated by Indians worldwide. It is also an occasion for all family members to gather for the ritual, bless the siblings and have a feast.

At the centre of the rituals is a thread, symbolizing the bond between sister and brother. The sister ties this thread, or rakhi, on the wrist of the brother, symbolizing a promise or commitment from the brother to forever care for his sister.

Over the ages, rakhis have transformed from being a mere red thread to elaborate bracelets, designer rakhis, stone or gems studded rakhis, silver or golden rakhis. Not only this, the tradition of the brother giving a gift to the sister has grown into a tender gesture.

The 1800giftportal, ever alert to gifting trends, has created several rakhi combos for Rakshabandhan.


  • ● Rakhi and flowers
  • ● Rakhis and cakes or chocolates
  • ● Rakhi and Indian mithai
  • ● Dry fruits, nuts and flowers

The rakhi gifts for sisters can be picked up from a vast range – apparel, fashion accessories, make-up kits, pieces of jewelry, cosmetics, hobby kits and so on.
